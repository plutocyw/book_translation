# Translation style guide

1. Use Traditional Chinese characters and natural Taiwan usage.
2. Use full-width Chinese punctuation in Chinese prose.
3. Preserve paragraph boundaries unless Chinese readability clearly requires a change.
4. Preserve emphasis, quotations, footnotes, and section hierarchy.
5. Translate meaning and voice, not English syntax mechanically.
6. Do not add explanations that are absent from the source.
7. Use approved glossary and character-register forms exactly.
8. If a source phrase is genuinely ambiguous, preserve the ambiguity where possible and flag it in the review record.
